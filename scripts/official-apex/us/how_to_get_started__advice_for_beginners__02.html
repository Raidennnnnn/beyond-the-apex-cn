Getting to Know the Width of Your Car

Learning to use the full width of the track

Now that you've familiarized yourself with your controller, let's work on developing your spatial awareness. Even when you're driving in "Cockpit View," it's important to have a sense of the width of your car. In sports driving, you must use the full width of the road, as in a race you can overtake (or be overtaken by) rival cars with the narrowest of openings.

Playing with your camera angle should help you to develop a feel for the width of your car in a race.

Once you've entered a track, select the 'Chase View' setting and drive your car to the edge of the road, so that your outer wheels are positioned on the white line. With your car stopped, change to the racing view that you would normally use when playing and study where the white line appears on the screen. If you are in 'Cockpit View', try to remember where the white line is relative to your window. Do this on both sides of the track.

Select 'Chase View', drive to the edge of the track, then stop.
While stopped, try switch the view. This is 'Cockpit View'.
This is 'Normal View'. Once you've checked one side of the track, go and do the other side.

These white lines mark the maximum track width available to you in a race. As I mentioned before, you will need to use the entire track in sports driving. This is especially true of corners, where an Out-In-Out* driving line will increase your turn radius and reduce the centrifugal force on your car. In such a case, knowing how close you can maneuver your car to the edge of the road is very important. To be competitive, you will want to develop a good spatial awareness of your car.

* Out-In-Out
A cornering method whereby you enter a corner on the outside of the track, cut in towards the middle, and exit the corner on the outside. This will result in a larger turning radius. Although it covers a greater distance, this method will reduce the centrifugal force on your car, allowing you to increase your turning speed.
