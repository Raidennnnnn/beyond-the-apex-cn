In-Game: General Principles

Keeping the accelerator depressed for as long as possible

One of the key things to bear in mind when adjusting settings is this: drivers that can keep the accelerator depressed for longer tend to win.

For example, to increase the top speed at the end of a straight, it's necessary to increase the exit speed at the preceding corner—and to do that, you need to start accelerating at an earlier point. The key to winning is therefore to consider the whole track and to work out how to spend as much time as possible with your accelerator depressed.

If you just think about one corner at a time, you won't win. You need to ensure that your car is as quick as possible around the track as a whole.

● Pay attention to your performance points

All the cars you can drive in Gran Turismo 7 have a 'PP' (performance points) rating. A car's PP is an indicator of its performance, and is used to group cars together for racing, among other things.

A car's PP is determined by key factors such as its top speed, acceleration, and RPM at various speeds (80, 120, and 160km/h). Improving these areas through tuning will also increase the car's PP.

If you're entering races with PP restrictions, don't just think of how to adjust PP in general—think of which specific PP-related factors would be best to adjust. For example, on tracks with several long straights you need to priorities top speed, which means boosting engine power. If you're racing on tricky short tracks, the focus is on maneuverability at low speeds, which will mean stiffening your suspension. Even cars with the same PP can have very different characteristics, so it's important to make sure you prepare your car for the event it's entering.
