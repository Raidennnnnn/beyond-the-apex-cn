Getting Used to Your Controller

Hi there, I'm YAM! My story with Gran Turismo goes back to my early school days when my eyes were first opened to the thrill of driving cars. After many years of honing my skills, and trying to hit ever faster speeds, I took home the Gran Turismo Asia Championship title in 2012.
From then on, I began working on the development side of Gran Turismo, while following the careers of the world's best players at Gran Turismo World Series events. Today I'd like to pass on some tips and tricks to new drivers of Gran Turismo.

Becoming one with your car

The first thing that new drivers will need to do is familiarize themselves with their controller. Although I prefer to race with a steering wheel and pedals, most newcomers to Gran Turismo 7 will be starting off with a wireless controller. Getting your wireless controller to react exactly how you want it to is the first step to improving your driving.

With that in mind, I'd like you to pick up your wireless controller. You do not need to grip or squeeze it tightly. Rather, you should hold it lightly, and try to play in a relaxed manner.

The first time you launch Gran Turismo 7, you will be given the option to choose from three different control styles: Left stick, Directional buttons, and Motion sensor. I recommend selecting the Motion sensor setting. Holding your controller in both hands, you will be able to tilt it left and right, just like a real steering wheel, which I think you'll find to be more natural.

The motion sensor function allows you to turn as if you were turning the steering wheel of a car

Similarly, there are three different pedal (accelerate/brake) control styles. Players who previously selected the Motion sensor setting will find it easiest to press the R2 and L2 buttons with their index fingers. In this control style, the R2 button is used for accelerating and the L2 button for braking.

I recommend 'R2/L2 buttons' which lets you use each index finger to control accelerating and braking

The amount of pressure that you apply to the R2 and L2 buttons will produce a corresponding reaction. In other words, by pressing the buttons lightly you will achieve a weak brake/acceleration, and by pressing them firmly, you will achieve a sharp brake/acceleration. Getting a good feel for decelerating and accelerating is something that you can only acquire by actually driving, so this may require some practice.

Try not to drive too fast at first. Instead, pay attention to how your car reacts to the steering and pedal settings you have selected. You will have succeeded when you no longer feel that you are merely 'controlling' your car but have become one with it.
