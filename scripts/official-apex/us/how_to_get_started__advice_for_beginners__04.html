Learning How to Be Precise

Reacting to where your racing marker is on screen

In the previous section, I talked about setting racing markers, but here I'd like to talk a little bit more about gauging speed.

A player once said to me, "I know how to find braking point markers, but I don't know exactly when to brake, as the position of my markers keeps changing according to my view." This player is absolutely right. Simply finding a marker will not tell you exactly when to brake.

Therefore, it is necessary that one create their own rules for reacting to racing markers.

Personally, I wait for the moment when the marker disappears from my view on the screen. Of course, this doesn't always work out straight away, so I need to make adjustments in some cases. This approach improves your accuracy one step at a time, bringing increased precision to your movements and more consistency to your lap times.

An example from just before the Fuji Speedway's Coca-Cola Corner. Wait for the moment when the distance marker disappears from view to start braking.

Being precise is not only important for responding to track markers, but also for accelerating and braking in general.

I once knew a player who was having trouble improving their top speed. They had made all kinds of adjustments to their car, but, in the end, the problem was that they were not pressing hard enough on the throttle. This happened at an event where steering wheel and pedal controllers were being used; because the player was wearing thick-soled trainers, they weren't able to judge precisely how much pressure they were applying to their foot pedal.
