The Angle

Photographing Cars 1

Eye-level is Often the Best Viewing Angle

I’ve been photographing cars for over forty years now. In this chapter, I’d like to share some of the things I’ve learned. I hope you’ll be able to use these hints to get more out of the ‘Scapes’ feature of Gran Turismo 7.

There are six major factors to bear in mind when taking photos of cars.

The first of these is the angle.

Start by finding the angle from which the car looks best. If you’re photographing a car in real life , I suggest that you take a few steps back, then slowly walk all the way around it, examining it from every side.

Using the ‘Scapes’ feature, it’s basically the same — place the car against your chosen background, then rotate the camera around it slowly. You’ll soon see that changing the angle even slightly will change features such as the outline of the wings and the curve of the bonnet. Pay attention to these subtle differences and find your favorite angle.

To find your preferred angle, start by looking at the car from a few different points of view.
Try to get a feel for what the car is ‘expressing’ — say, through the shape of its silhouette or the undulations of its body.

If you like, you can even try finding your favored angle first, then choosing your background — in other words, the opposite method to the one described above. This isn’t really something you can do in real life, so it’s kind of a new way of thinking: one that ‘Scapes’ has made possible.

Choosing an angle also involves choosing a height from which to photograph the car. For example, you can choose to look down from above or up from below. Changing the height from which you take a photograph can really change the feel of the shot. It’s worth noting that, in the past, car designers would carve models of their designs out of wood. Because they used to place these models on their desks and shelves, the cars always ended up being viewed, and looking their best, mainly from above. Later on, it became easier to make full-size clay models of cars; as a result, the level of the human eye became the height from which their appearance was best judged. These days, of course, cars are designed using CAD, and can be looked at from any angle. For me, however, eye-level remains the best angle from which to look at a car.

In the past, the process of designing cars involved making scale models. As a result, cars of a certain vintage often have charms that only reveal themselves when viewed from above.

Developing an appreciation of the differences between photographs taken from a range of angles is crucial for any aspiring car photographer.
