Using Driver Assist ① - Braking

Knowing when to brake and adjust your speed

Gran Turismo 7 includes Driver Assist functions designed to help beginners get comfortable behind the wheel. We recommend making full use of these features, as they can be the quickest way to build confidence and improve your driving skills. Let’s begin by looking at the assist functions related to braking.

"Driver Assist" will be enabled for players who select the "Beginner" or "Intermediate" assist preset when starting the game for the first time. The available functions and level of assistance will vary depending on the preset selected. You can change this setting from the "Driving Assistant" option in the "Quick Menu" that appears before each race.

● Braking area

Drivers who brake too early will slow down more than necessary, while those who brake too late may have trouble getting through corners cleanly. As mentioned earlier, setting braking markers can help, even if it feels difficult at first.

To help with this, we recommend using the "Braking Area" assist. This feature shows where you should begin braking by displaying a red line on the road. Once your car enters the "Braking Area," apply the brakes.

As your speed drops such that you can clear the corner, the red line will start to fade. From then on, you'll need to be careful with your acceleration. Try to maintain your speed until the end of the corner is in sight. Let off the throttle if you feel that you're going off-track.

The braking area is a series of red lines across the track which indicate the point at which you should apply the brake.

● Braking indicator

There is another method for learning when to brake. This involves using the 'Braking Indicator' meter.

As you approach a braking point, the center of the meter will turn red. Be ready to brake as soon as the meter begins flashing. Like the "Braking Area," the "Braking Indicator" shows when you need to slow down, so choose the method that works best for you.

The center of the meter will turn red to indicate when it's time to brake.

● Suggested gear

Most beginners use automatic transmission, so they do not need to worry about shifting gears. Even so, there is one assist function worth considering: "Suggested Gear."

Located next to your current gear, the suggested gear is the gear you should be in for an upcoming corner. This will give you a general idea of the speed needed to clear the corner depending on the gear you're in, and will serve as a guide for how much you need to slow down. Keep it in mind.

The section of the meter surrounded by the red square shows the suggested gear. Fourth gear is currently engaged, but it's suggested that the driver switches to third gear ahead of the corner.

● Auto-brake / auto-drive

These functions are designed to support players who are still developing their braking and cornering skills. "Auto-Brake" assists with deceleration, while "Auto-Drive" helps with steering through corners, giving beginners a feel for what a smooth driving line should be. Of course, these functions will not do everything for you, so try not to become too dependent on them. Always remember that you are the driver, and you are the one in control behind the wheel.

The Auto-Brake and Auto-Drive assists will help you with your braking and steering. These features can be adjusted from the Quick Menu.
