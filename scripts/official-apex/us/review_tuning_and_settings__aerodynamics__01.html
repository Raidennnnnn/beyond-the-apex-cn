Aerodynamic Tuning

Aerodynamic parts are often added for styling, but when used correctly they play an important role in optimizing performance. Well-designed aerodynamics can reduce drag and control lift, improving high-speed stability. The downforce generated by these components increases tire load and helps maximize grip and controllability. However, aerodynamic tuning must be balanced with the suspension and overall vehicle setup, as poor implementation can reduce performance.

Front Spoiler

These components are designed to control airflow beneath the car and reduce lift. However, if poorly designed or improperly installed, they can disrupt airflow and increase pressure under the body, reducing stability. In extreme cases, this can lead to a significant loss of control.

Rear Bumper Spoiler

These components are designed to shape airflow around the rear bumper, helping to reduce turbulence and improve overall aerodynamic efficiency. They can be integrated into the bumper as a single unit or added as a separate attachment beneath it. The integrated type is often referred to as a rear bumper spoiler, while the add-on type is commonly called a rear under spoiler or rear skirt.

Side Spoiler

Also known as side skirts or side steps, these are mounted along the side sills of the body. They help manage airflow along the sides of the car and can reduce turbulence.

Rear Wing

Mounted on the upper rear of the body, rear wings help manage airflow and generate downforce. Their shape is designed to reduce lift and improve high-speed stability. Larger wings can produce greater downforce, increasing load on the rear tires and improving grip, although this is often accompanied by increased drag.

Rear Diffuser

Rear diffusers are fitted beneath the rear of the car and work by accelerating airflow from the underbody, helping to lower pressure and generate downforce. They are widely used in racing applications, and their effectiveness depends on factors such as ride height and overall underbody airflow.
