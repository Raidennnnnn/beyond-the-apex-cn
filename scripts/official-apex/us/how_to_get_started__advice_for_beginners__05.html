Taking Corners

Pay attention to when you start turning

Lastly, I'd like to touch on taking corners.

Remember the out-in-out method I mentioned previously? When cornering, the basic premise is to drive along an arc that goes from the outside to the inside, and then back to the outside of the track.

Start by slowly steering into a corner from the outside of the track, and continue steering inwards until you reach the midpoint of the corner. At the innermost point (clipping point or apex), check to see that your steering angle is at its maximum. From there, you'll need to begin to correct your steering (i.e. return it to normal.) If you can do this, then you'll have made a perfect turn.

In Gran Turismo 7, corner indicators can help you with your cornering. These will appear in yellow on your screen. Linking one corner indicator to the next will help you understand the line you should be taking.

If you find that your car is reaching the inside of the track before you'd like it to, you may be starting your turn too early. Conversely, if you aren't able to reach the inside of the track by the midpoint of the corner, you may be starting your turn too late. Although your driving line will be affected by speed and other variables, you should focus first on finding the point at which you need to start turning.

While cornering, it is also important that you continuously adjust your line of sight. In addition to paying attention to your braking marker, your steering marker, the clipping point of the corner, and the exit of the corner, you should always be thinking one step ahead of your present position. By keeping an eye out for what's up ahead, you'll be able to see your way through the entire corner.
